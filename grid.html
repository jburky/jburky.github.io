Proposal

Municipal services, such as medical assistance, could be much more responsive and efficient with the advent of connected communities that can sense medical emergencies as they arise and deploy assistance appropriately. Imagine your daughter experiencing an anaphylactic reaction to walnuts and your phone being unable to connect to 911.

This happened to me when my daughter was 20 months old. I ran next door frantically to try to borrow my neighbor’s phone. To my surprise and relief, my neighbor was a nurse who immediately grabbed a bottle of Benadryl and quickly administered a large dose to my daughter, which luckily, was able to alleviate her respiratory distress. At that same time, I was finally able to connect to 911. Once the ambulance and medics arrived, they explained how timely intervention by my neighbor probably saved my daughter’s life. This kind of community response in the future could help decrease morbidity and mortality in communities of any size by enabling connectivity and better communication.
